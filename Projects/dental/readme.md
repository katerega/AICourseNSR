THE 15-MINUTE DEMO SCRIPT
Minute 0–2: Frame Control

“Dr. [Name], thanks for taking 15 minutes.

This won’t be a software demo.
I just want to identify if there’s hidden revenue in missed calls, delayed treatment plans, or no-shows — and if fixing it makes sense for you.”

Now ask:

How many implant/cosmetic consults do you do per month?

Roughly how many convert?

Do you track how many accepted cases delay or don’t pay?

Let them talk.

Minute 3–6: The Math (This Is The Close Setup)

Use simple numbers.

Example:

“If you see 40 consults/month and even 5 delay or disappear,
and your average case is $3,000,
that’s $15,000 sitting uncollected.”

Pause.

“Even recovering 2 of those per month is $6,000.”

Now they are thinking money.

Not software.

Minute 7–10: The System (Keep It Simple)

“We install a lightweight follow-up system that:

• Immediately responds to missed calls
• Follows up on unpaid treatment plans
• Reminds no-shows automatically
• Re-engages ‘I’ll think about it’ patients

It runs automatically, so your front desk doesn’t have to chase patients manually.”

Do NOT show 50 features.
Do NOT over-explain.

Keep it revenue-focused.

Minute 11–13: Qualification Close

“Let me ask — if we could help you recover even 1–3 additional cases per month, would that be meaningful for you?”

They will say yes.

Then:

“Are you currently doing anything structured to recover delayed treatment plans?”

If no → strong opportunity
If yes → you pivot (see objection section below)

Minute 14–15: Transition to Close

“Based on what you’ve told me, there’s likely revenue being left on the table.

Would you like me to explain how we implement this and what the investment looks like?”

Wait for yes.

Now move to close.

🔹 THE $1,000 CLOSING PITCH

Keep this calm and logical.

“Implementation is straightforward.

We set up the follow-up automation, integrate with your existing booking or CRM, customize the messaging to match your clinic tone, and monitor performance.

Setup is completed within 7 days.

The investment is a one-time $1,000.

If this recovers even one additional implant case, it pays for itself immediately.”

Pause.

Silence is powerful.

Then:

“Would you like to move forward and get this installed this week?”

Stop talking.

🔹 OBJECTION HANDLING
1️⃣ “We already have a system.”

Response:

“That’s good. Most clinics do.

The question isn’t whether you have a system — it’s whether it actively re-engages delayed high-ticket treatment plans automatically.

Are your follow-ups fully automated beyond the first reminder?”

Let them answer.

Then:

“We’re not replacing your system.
We’re plugging the revenue gaps most systems ignore.”

2️⃣ “We’ll think about it.”

“Of course.

Usually when clinics say that, it’s either timing or budget.

Which one is it for you?”

Let them answer.

Then:

“If timing — when would you realistically want to address revenue leakage?”

“If budget — would recovering even one case make this comfortable?”

Make them confront the math.

3️⃣ “Send more information.”

“Happy to.

Before I do — is there anything specific holding you back right now?”

Don’t just send info and disappear.

4️⃣ “Too expensive.”

Calm response:

“If this recovered just one $3,000–$5,000 case, would it still feel expensive?”

Re-anchor to revenue.
